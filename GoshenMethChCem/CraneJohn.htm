GOSHEN CEMETERY aka Goshen Methodist Church Cemetery, (west) MAURY COUNTY TENNESSEE
CRANE, Rev. John, 1787 - 14 Feb 1813. "New Monument erected by the United Methodist Church; Historic Site No. 86; "Born 1787 in a Fort two miles below Nashville, Tenn. Admitted into the Tennessee Conference, Methodist Church, in 1807. Served circuits in North Carolina, East Tennessee, Ohio, Louisiana, Missouri, Kentucky, Duck River Circuit in 1812. He was the first member to die in the Tennessee Conference. The first to be buried in Goshen Cemetery. Died Feb 14, 1813." Old stone: "Jnd. (Joined) Conf. 1807." "1st. Burial at Goshen." Old John Crane monument / (New Stone United Methodist Historic Site plaque attached to stone. s/o Lewis (Crane) Crain and Mary Polly Lindsay Crane; http://www.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~tnsumner/falcr.htm
John born in 1787. He was one of the famous child evangelists during the religious revival after the New Madrid earthquake. At the age of 12 he began preaching the gospel and died in his early 20's as a circuit riding preacher on the Green River circuit.)
