Mhoontown Cemetery
Colbert County, Alabama

Lat. 34� 49˘ 19˛ N, Lon. 87� 56˘ 40˛ W

To reach this cemetery, from Cherokee, Alabama, turn north off highway 72 W. onto Main St. Go to stop sign and turn right (east) onto Old Highway 72/ Old Lee Hwy. Go about 1 block and turn left (north) onto North Pike Rd. and go across the railroad tracks. Travel 4.4 miles to Mhoontown road. Turn right onto Mhoontown road and travel about 1.1 mile to the church and cemetery.

From Florence, Alabama, travel west on Alabama Hwy. 20 for 3.3 miles. Turn left onto County Rd. 14 (Waterloo Rd.) and go 11.9 miles and turn right onto access approach to Natchez Trace Parkway. Turn right (south) onto Natchez Trace Parkway and go 5.7 miles. Turn left off the Parkway and turn right (south) onto North Pike Rd. Go .4 mile and turn left onto Mhoontown Rd. Travel about 1.1 mile to the church. The cemetery is to the right and south of the church.

This cemetery is in the far northwestern end of Colbert County, Alabama. The cemetery was established in the early 1700�s and is owned & maintained by Mhoontown United Methodist Church. The oldest person buried here was born in 1729. There are about 45 unknown graves in the cemetery. According to a Mr. Bob Ables, the church was established around 1828. The cemetery is in good condition and still in use. As most old cemeteries are, the grave plots are misaligned somewhat. There is some room left in this cemetery for future burials. The cemetery sign honors deceased Manard Askew for his efforts in establishing the cemetery maintenance fund.

All information has been recorded from the head stones and/or foot stones and has been checked for accuracy. This survey was completed on November 28, 2003. All names and dates have been checked for accuracy.
