OLD BEAR CREEK CEMETERY, MAURY COUNTY 1807-1836, MARSHALL COUNTY Aft. 1836.


 The old home place where once they lived. 
They reared their families. Time passed and they grew old. 
As mortals their bodies gave way to decline, and that sad chariot eventually came to visit.
Each of them took that final trip to the family cemetery upon the nearby hill.
A large fieldstone was placed at the head of each of the graves for future reference.
One day news came of the traveling Memorial Caravan
from Memphis and/or St Louis visiting Mooresville or Culleoka.
The sons & maybe daughters went there to meet the expected visitor. 
Together they drew up plans for a grand monument.
Soon the memorial arrived by wagon & a team of mules.
The next generation gathered around one day and the monument was set lovingly in place, 
its purpose to cherish & preserve many memories of the past.
Gods plan of the spawning and passing of the generation prevails, and all is soon forgotten. 
We have today remaining names and words on a stone beautifully inscribed.
So with great awareness of what is, we labor here to preserve a small part of what was,
 knowing that with God's plan there will be a joyous reunion someday. C.W. Austin, 2005

Mr. Hawkins assessment in 1987:
This old cemetery has been abandoned for many years. There is now a fence around it but it is evident that cattle have used the cemetery for a bedding area in years past. Many monuments have been tumbled, broken and dislocated. There were once a number of large cedar trees in the lot but three or four have been struck by lightening and one had fallen over the stones. Fortunately, no monuments were
damaged. 

My assessment in 2008:
The fence is down again and the livestock roam freely among the memorials. Many of the memorials are in serious decay and without a serious effort at restoration will be gone in a few years. I would hope that this site will serve as one more tool to include you in the effort to impede that decline.
