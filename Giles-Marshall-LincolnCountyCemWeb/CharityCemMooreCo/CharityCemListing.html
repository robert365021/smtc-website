Unknown Souls: 3691/3714/3715/3720
Unknown soul, A bird fluttering in the wind, hovering over a lost (fieldstone marked) grave. (The stone is fallen and no longer stands. At one time it stood on its end and was buried about half way into the ground and facing east with its back to the west as Christians did. Tombstones only came into wide spread use in the south about 1820. Subsequent economic hard times often determined whether there was a gravestone with formally inscribed markers even after that (especially for rural farmers): 3812

The area inside this rock wall is very large and the graves that occupy it only fill about 1/3 of the space. That leads me to believe that at one time there was a either a church building inside this rock wall or we have several hundred unmarked graves. If there weren't unmarked graves filling up this space to some degree current residents would have gotten the word and used the remaining space as a modern cemetery. The marked stones that are here are in well aligned rows indicating a land survey laying out the graves existed at one time. Therefore I am inclined to believe the early Charity Church sat next to the graveyard inside the rock wall on the east side of the cemetery. If you have knowledge of this please share it with the public by writing me at Wayneal1@aol.com
