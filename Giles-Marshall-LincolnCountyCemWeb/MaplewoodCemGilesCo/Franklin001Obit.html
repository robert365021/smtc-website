MAPLEHILL CEMETERY, Pulaski, GILES COUNTY TENNESSEE


FRANKLN, Benjamin Dunn, 1 Jan 1807 - 2 Jun 1897, Pulaski Giles Co Tn. Maplewood Cemetery (according to the Obit.. Pulaski Tennessee where he is interred and has a write up.

"Mr. Benjamin (Dunn) Franklin, one of Giles County's oldest and most respected citizens died at the home of his daughter, Mrs. J. E. Matthews, Wednesday, June 2, at 10:30 a.m. in the 91st year of his age.
He was born in eastern Georgia, Jan. 1, 1807, and came to Tennessee with his grandfather, Abraham Franklin, in 1818. His first wife was Miss Francis (Dorothy) Lee. His second wife was Miss America Jones who died in 1875. There were no children of his last marriage.
 
His surviving children are Mrs. Sarah Ann (David Lanier) Graves, of Neosho, MO.; Mrs. N(ewton). G(reene). Eslick (interred in New Zion Cemetery) and Mrs. J. E. Matthews, of and near this place. He was a life-long Methodist and an old line Whig.

After funeral services at the residence by Rev. Marion Kennedy, the remains were interred in Maplewood."

This Ben Franklin family was Family #794 in Dist 10 of Giles County 1860. This was next door to the Samuel family who were Family #795. There is an old unmarked Franklin Cemetery just west of Fire-tower Road back in the woods with stones without any inscription. Since my efforts to find more of this family listed in graveyards of Giles County have failed I have to concluded they are buried in this lost Franklin cemetery in unmarked graves. Perhaps buried there are the children of Ben & Sarah Franklin that died in their home in Dist 10 along with possible earlier generations such as the grandfather and parents mentioned below. Statement says that Ben came to Giles Co with his grandfather Abraham Franklin not mentioning the parents.
