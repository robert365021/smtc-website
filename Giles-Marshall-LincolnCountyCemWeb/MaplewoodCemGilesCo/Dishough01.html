1893    Arrena ELDER vs W. I. RAINEY et al

DISHOUGHN brought suit April 16, 1878 in Circuit Court.  Augustine DISHOUGHN died August 20, 1877, testate.  Left property to son Charles A. DISHOUGHN.  Arrena DISHOUGHN married Joseph ELDER.  W. I. RAINEY sold his mill to Alexander KIDWELL.

1902   Arrena ELDER vs Walter B. and Thomas J. CLEGG

On September 2, 1885, a case against Arrena ELDER was brought by Thomas A. PITMAN, by his next friend Thomas W. PITMAN, claiming an interest in certain property left by her first husband Augustine DISHOUGHN.  Property included the DISHOUGHN Mill, the Mill lot on which the old James N. BUNCH Mill stood and which DISHOUGHN bought January 25,1869.  Note:Mrs. Arrena DISHOUGHN had two children by Augustine DISHOUGHN: Charles A. and George Ann DISHOUGHN.  George Ann married T. W. PITMAN.

 1888  Arena ELDER vs Thomas A. PITMAN

Augustine DISHOUGHN died leaving widow Arena and two children: Georgeann who married Thomas W. PITMAN and Charles DISHOUGHN and that both children are now dead.  Georgeann was mother of Thomas A. PITMAN.  Arena DISHOUGHN married second, Joseph ELDER.  Mrs. ELDER received 1/3 of DISHOUGHN estate.  Thomas A. PITMAN received 2/3.

1885 Will of Augustine DISHOUGH  Thomas A. PITTMAN by next friend vs. Arena DISHOUGH

Augustine DISHOUGH died August 1877. He left surviving his widow Arrena, son Charles A., and daughter George Ann wife of Thomas A. PITTMAN. Under the Will of Augustine DISHOUGH he gave his wife Arrena all of his real and personal property for use and control until son Charles A., arrived at the age of twenty one. Then property to be divided. between wife, son and daughter. George Ann wife of Thomas W. PITTMAN, died intestate in 1884 leaving Thomas A. PITTMAN who was a minor of tender years. Charles A. DISHOUGH died a few days after George Ann, leaving no issue, never having been married. Thomas W. PITTMAN was suing as next friend of Thomas A. PITTMAN, claiming he should share in the interest of Charles A. DISHOUGH as well as George Ann PITTMAN. Thomas W. PITTMAN asked that a Guardian be appointed to protect the interest of Thomas A. PITTMAN.

NOTE: Arena BUSBY married Augustine DISHOUGH III, July 4, 1855

1887 Noble SMITHSON vs. Arrena ELDER

Augustine DISHOUGH published his will August 18, 1877. After the date of said will, he died leaving his widow Arrena and two children: Georgeann wife of T. A. PITMAN and Charles A. DISHOUGH surviving. Charles A. DISHOUGH died February 8, 1884, Georgeann PITMAN died February 3, 1884 leaving a son Thomas PITMAN.
