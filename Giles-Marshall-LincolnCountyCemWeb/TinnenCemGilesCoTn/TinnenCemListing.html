There are several field stone markers without names and dates. It is unknown to me if any of these were slave graves, but the Tinnen's did not report any slaves in 1830. Because of that I assume there were none here and all these graves are for Tinnen family members: 9908/9909/9910/9911/9912/9913/9914/9915/9916

** After studying the censuses, Ancestry.com & these two markers I conclude the children who are in John L. Tinnen's home after Teressa Simpson Tinnen died in 1852 are those of Nancy (?) Tinnen John's his 2nd wife. He evidently remarried to Nancy the 2nd wife and she died sometime after son Tom W. Tinnen was born who was then 4 years old. Tom was born about 1876 as shown in the 1880 census.  Nancy the 2nd wife would have died about 1877 and probably lies in an unmarked grave in this cemetery. The number of graves in this cemetery marked only with fieldstones without inscriptions were enough to cause me to attempt to add*** Nancy to the above listing using raw research. I did not find a credible marriage record for Nancy & John L Tinnen but it was between 1852 & 1860.


There are many ways to spell this surname. I have chosen to stay with what is spelled out on Teressa Tinnen's tombstone. I have seen this more predominantly as Tinnon with the "o". See Robert Tinnon Cem across African Hollow.
