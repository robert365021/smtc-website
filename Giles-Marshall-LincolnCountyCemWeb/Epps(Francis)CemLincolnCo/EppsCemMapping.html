EPPS FAMILY GRAVES, Hughey Rd, LINCOLN COUNTY TENNESSEE
I made an assumption that they took the bodies down the residential lane and buried them on the north side of the road in graves were there were flat and unclear of rock out cropping where they could find soil.  This tradition usually started with small children that died young even at birth  and established a family graveyard that way. This is also based on the topographical maps of the 1940s showing the farm/residential road back to this place. It terminated at the home place where I have the label for the home in yellow.

This map shows two checks where Mr. Barnes says there may be an old unmarked cemetery somewhere. He was not conclusive. Maybe it is further up the hill. This is a map showing the complete hollow (Epps) After evaluation of the topographical features of the two locations I have concluded the check on the right is just rough terrain and that is why it is not cleared land.Not sure the left check is still on Epps land.
