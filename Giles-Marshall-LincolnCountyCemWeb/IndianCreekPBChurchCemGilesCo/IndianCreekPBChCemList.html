History source: GI Co Cem book 1987: This is the third location of this church. The original church was organized in 1811 and was on land from Isham Brown. It was moved about 1834 - 1840 two miles west of the first location on a hill on land later owned by James Ed. Sanders. The last and present location was about 1881, where is now stands, still on Indian Creek and about two miles from the former location.
It has been reported that at least one of the above locations contained a cemetery but no one in the Historical Society found or reported it in 1987. Maybe it is still there maybe it is lost or maybe it never had formal tombstones which almost always guarantees it will be destroyed especially if it is on productive land. If not on productive land it will be usually be abandoned and lost in the bush of today's jungle greenery from excess rainfall. I have seen vegetation so bad in a couple of instances that one had to be standing within 10 feet to spot a lost tombstone even if it was standing or not.
