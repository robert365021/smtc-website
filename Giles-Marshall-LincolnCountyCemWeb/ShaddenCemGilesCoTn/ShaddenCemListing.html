CWA Comment: 27 Apr 2017:
I am not guaranteeing that Martin Shadden was Alice's husband but a marriage record existed in Ancestry.com for Ailcey Dodson marrying Martin Shadden in 10 Jul 1807. I cannot find the 1820 census on this family, but 1830 has Ashley/(Alice) Shadden and the 1840 census another name Alcy. She is known in other censuses as Alice so deviations for her given name occurred as she progressed thru those records. So by 1830 Alice was a widow with children if I cut thru the errors and read the census correctly.

Censuses are no help here because actually the baby listed on the stone was between them and the listing is before the censuses started enumerating names of children in 1850. Photographed on a damp Monday 24 Apr 2017. Archie Williams provided access using his four wheel drive truck and helped me find and survey this cemetery. The cemetery is about a mile behind his home. He is the caretaker of this land and now operates a cattle farm with his wife. I was pleased to find that the cattle do not generally come this far back into the woods probably due to the rocky hillside land and woodland.
