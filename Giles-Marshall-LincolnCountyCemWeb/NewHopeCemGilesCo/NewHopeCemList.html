Fieldstones probably marking the head or foot of a grave: 8185 / 8192
A grave under the White Pine Tree marked with a stone and wooden cross with a dislocated footstone bearing the initials S. F or possibly S. H. or S. E. The footstone has been hit by a plow in the past which diminished the readability of the last (surname) letter. 8280 / 8281 / 8282

Could this be the Slave section: no visible monuments, but appears to be graves with a buried rock lined area. It is also possible it is the area used by white settlers before stones began to be used in about 1815. It is stated in the Giles Co Cemetery book that there is a slave area in this cemetery: 8200 / 8201
