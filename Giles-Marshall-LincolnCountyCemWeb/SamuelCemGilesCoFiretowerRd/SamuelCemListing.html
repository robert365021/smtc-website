Source for this listing is now basedon a visit and photography session Tombstone inscriptions and the Giles County Tennessee Cemetery book, by the Giles County Historical Society 1985, page 475 and census records on Ancestry.com. Added here 1st 5 Mar 2013 by C. Wayne Austin. Revised 21 Apr 2016 for a visit which took place 19 Apr 2016. It is obvious from the photos that this cemetery has been abandoned for years. Only two of the stones are broken and require special repairs to get the cemetery standing up. The other stones could be reset into the ground and will stand. It would do no good to stand them up and watch the cattle break/mash them back into the ground with their rubbing action. Fencing without further maintenance would allow giant trees to spring up and eventually fall down on the stones in storms so that is a failed option in the long run. I propose a narrow fenced area with barb wire without the barbs that allows the cattle to stick their heads in and graze the cemetery from across the fence to keep the vegetation under control without touching the stones otherwise.
