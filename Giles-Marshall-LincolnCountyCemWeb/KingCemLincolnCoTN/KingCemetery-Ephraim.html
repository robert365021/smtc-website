Julia Wright Molitz - Ephraim King notes are  from: "Petersburg History" by Heritage Committee "Homecoming 86" & census information and other as mentioned:

"Epraim King, a brother of Sara King who married John Armstrong, came to Petersburg with his brothers and sisters and his mother. Ephraim King was born about 1789 in South Carolina. He married Jane Harkins and died in 1845.

Their known children were:

1 James G. King II, Andrew Jackson King, born Sept. 8, 1813, in Tennessee. He married Isabelle Eliza Mitchell, Feb. 18, 1839. They were the grandparents of Ibbie Delk who married John and were the parents of Miss Mary Scott a retired school teacher. She has done the most extensive research on her family and on Petersburg.

2 Shadrack A. King, born March 22, 1819, married Mary McAdams September 16, 1840. Mary was born January 20, 1821. She was the daughter of John McAdams. Their daughter Rhetta Elizabeth King married Noah Benton Scott.

3 Epraim Caroll King - married Theodocia Burrow - died young from a horse injury.

4 Martha G. King first married William Sorrells on March 2, 1843, E.B. King, Justice of the Peace. Secord marriage to John Holley.

5 In 1850 census - Jane Harkins King, widow of Epraim King, Jane King Estate valued at $10,000.00.

6 Ephraim King bought these tracts of land East of Petersburg and Chestnut Ridge and West of Chestnut Ridge. 1817-14 acres for $60.00, 1834-100 acres for $300.00, 1835-80 acres for $265.00, 1836-394 acres for $1500.00, 1838-50 acres for $75.00."

Julia Wright Molitz - Jane Harkins King died before 13 Feb 1862. That is the date of court records were my ggrandfather James Wright had to sue her son my g-grandfather Andrew Jackson King (her executor) as Jane had died. Jane sold land to James Wright in 1859 but Jane did not have rights to the land. It went through the court for years because of the Civil War. During the War all papers of the court were lost. Therefore after the War James Wright had to start all over again with the suit. James Wright did get legal rights to the land. It was the family farm until he died in 1893. His land bordered Kings land to the north. I believe that Martha E King Armstrong (Andrew Jackson King's daughter & my great grandmother) lived on the King land with her 4 children once she became a widow. Therefore my grandparents James & Willie Etta Armstrong Wright were neighbors. Just a little romance speculation there LOL. James & Rebecca Renfrow Wright are buried in the Harkins cemetery. Many people believe Jane Harkins King died in 1863. But that was the date that some of her land was being sold. I have from research that others have done that Jane Harkin King was born 1789 Pendleton South Carolina.

Julia Wright Molitz -  Yes, it is fine with me to publish on your site. This morning I have gone over the many papers from this law suit. It is very involved. The last paper where Jane Harkins King is still alive is dated 27 Aug 1861. On 13 Feb 1862 is the first document where the court is filing the suit to Andrew Jackson King on behalf of James Wright. "Where as one James Wright of Lincoln County in the Chancery Court of said County, against one Jane King, And where as the said Jane King has departed this life......" The land belonged to John J Greer. Jane was to pay Greer with James Wright notes & notes from Charles Chesser who purchased James Wright's land in Norris Creek. She did not do so. Finally on 25 Nov 1866 James Wright purchase the land from John J Greer. Now the reason Jane does not appear on the 1860 census is because some of the dist 10 census is either missing or was not taken. James Wright does not appears on the 1860 census as well. I have been in contact with others who ancestors in dist 10 are missing.
