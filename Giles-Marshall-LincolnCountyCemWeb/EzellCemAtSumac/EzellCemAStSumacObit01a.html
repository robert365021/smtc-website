EZELL CEMETERY (Pulaski, Old Hwy 15 behind home), GILES COUNTY TENNESSEE

SAMUEL, William James,16 Apr 1840 - 17 Apr 1915, h/o Sarah Ann Ezell Samuel, s/o William Sanders and Dorinda French Anderson Samuel
Obituary: The Pulaski Citizen 22 Apr 1915

William James Samuel died Saturday at his home at Sumac aged about 75 years. Funeral services were conducted at the residence by Revs. Will Nelson and L. H. Laten, followed
by the burial at Zion Ezell by his old comrades of the John H. Woldridge Bivouac of which he was a member. Mr. Samuel was as good a soldier as ever shouldered a gun during
the civil strife of the 60's and was a member of the First Tennessee Regiment, Capt. Field's Company, later Capt W. C. Flournoy's. He was wounded at Chickamauga and also at
the last battle with Hood at Nashville. The wounds gave him pain as long as he lived. Mr. Samuel was a splendid type of the highest citizenship and was revered for his nobility
of character. His wife died some years ago and surviving him are the following children: Mesdames Lillie Thompson of Lewisburg, Grace Estes of Virginia, and Mrs. Eslick of Sumac and
Will Samuel of Decatur.
