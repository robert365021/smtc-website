Folks these old Turtle Back Vaults with the tombstones concreted in shows us a good lesson as to the reason why concrete should not be associated with old tombstone repair at least above ground. One can see several cases of the tombstones failing in this cemetery after they were plastered with concrete into the Turtle Back Vaults to form one unit. The difference in porous capacity of concrete versus these old tombstones causes varying water retention allowing freezing and thawing to differently affect the tombstone in contact with the concrete causing breakage of the softer lime tombstone. Seems the Vaults being also concreted to keep the water from seeping into them have fared better then the tombstones/footstones.
