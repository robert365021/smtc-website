Descendants of the slaves maintained the cemetery well into the 20th century, but as more and more have moved away and time has passed, it has long since become overgrown.  Now only the new black section is being routinely maintained. The fence has been felled by trees on occasion, but makeshift repairs have been made by someone. The gate is now barb wired shut, but it can be opened with care, or you can resort to a belly crawl or other agile means to get under it. I have not been there this summer but knowing we had a more than average wet season the weeds must be ten feet tall with privy hedge taking over the place.

It has been said that the cemetery can be seen briefly in the 1995 feature film "Tom and Huck."
