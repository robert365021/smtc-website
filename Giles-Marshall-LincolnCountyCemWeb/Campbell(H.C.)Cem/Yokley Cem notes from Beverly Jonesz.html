Yokley cemetery - at Hwy 245 (Campbellsville Pike) and Smith Hollow Road. It's up on a hill and is visible from Hwy 245. It's in the middle of a farm property/cattle pasture, but is fenced around the cemetery. Lots of graves in it.
I took pictures of as many as I could. Some were tipped over and too heavy to lift, and some were unmarked or marked with just a simple stone. It didn't have a sign or anything on the gate. There are some other families buried there as well.
 
The old cemetery book gives directions thusly:
Yokley Cemetery location:  Hwy 64W, right through Campbellsville to Columbia Road. Cemetery on right on hill behind Angus house, in chain link fence. 
 
Terina Boyd, who provided the photo of the stone for Emma Hayes, had this to say about Yokley Cemetery:
I could not get every grave as some were tipped over and too heavy to lift and see who was on the stone. Cattle have been in the cemetery and have tipped over/damaged several stones and it is somewhat grown up with grass and weeds. Whoever owns the farm has also been dragging their dead cows into the cemetery to rot, lots of remains scattered around. There was a fairly "fresh" one in there when I went in, so I didn't stay in there as long as I would like.
There were a few "box tombs" that were very worn or were damaged. Some I could read, and some not.
There were some unmarked graves or graves with just a plain rock and no name.
So I just took as many pictures as I could.
Needless to say, there are definitely a few more graves that still need to be identified in that cemetery. May have to go back in there again.
