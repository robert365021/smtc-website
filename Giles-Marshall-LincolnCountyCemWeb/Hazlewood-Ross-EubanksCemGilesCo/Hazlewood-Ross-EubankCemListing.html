This cemetery was well fenced. Unfortunately no one had cut the weeds in a while and it was hard to find the stones in the thick weeds. This is a predicament in that if you leave the cattle to roam the cemetery they will trample and break the stones. Fence it and the weeds take over if one does not cut them.

Visited & photographed 1 Nov 2012 by Wayne Austin. Added to this site 2 Nov 2012 by the same. Also published in the book Giles County Cemeteries, Page 171 by the Historical Society 1986. Also presented on Findagrave.com with less photos, but some genealogy.
